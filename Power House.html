Frequenctly Asked Questions

What is a solar power system?
A solar power system is a system that uses solar panels to convert sunlight into electricity. Solar power systems are used in residential, commercial, and industrial settings.
How does a solar power system work?
Solar panels collect sunlight and convert it into electrical energy. This electrical energy is used to power your home or business. When the sun isn’t shining the energy can be stored in batteries, or sent back to the electrical grid where you are credited per kWh supplied.
How much does a solar power system cost?
The cost of a solar power system depends on a number of factors, including the size of the system, the type of panels used, and the location of the installation. However, the average cost of a complete solar power system ranges from $1 -$10 per watt installed.
Are there any government incentives available for solar power systems?
Yes! The federal government offers tax credits for both residential and commercial installations of solar power systems. In addition, many states offer their own incentives for solar energy projects.
Am I still connected to the grid after I install solar? What happens when there is no sun?

Yes. When installing a solar energy system the customer receives a smart meter that is connected, both to the solar system and the grid. When the sun is shining the building will withdraw power from the solar system and if the solar system is producing more than the building consumes the meter will go backwards earning the customer credits. At night, when the building uses power, it will use the grid as usual and will use the credits it earned during the day.

I don’t own the building. I’m only a tenant. Can I still go solar?
Just like other building improvements, a solar energy system is a long-term asset, with long-term payoffs. If you have a long-term lease and plan to stay in the building for a long time, then it will depend on the following factors: do you have roof rights in your leasing contracts and will your landlord be cooperative? For the landlord, it is worthwhile to give you permission and to get a free improvement for the building that increases the property’s value. SolarKal can help you find a solution, even if you don’t own the building.
What happens if, after installing solar panels in my roof, the roof leaks?
There are no holes being drilled in your roof during installation of the solar panels. In fact, the panels block the UV lights that are the #1 reason for roof degradation so you are extending the life of your roof. In case there is a roof leak in the roof, you will call your roofer as you would do otherwise. All roofers know how to work with solar energy systems.
I’ve heard the efficiency of the panels keeps getting better. What happens if there is a breakthrough in 5 years?
Solar panels are getting cheaper and more efficient every year. However, silicon solar panels have a peak technical efficiency that we are approaching, meaning many of the efficiency gains have already been achieved. Additionally, the economics of solar are in a unique position right now, as solar prices have fallen considerably but federal and state incentives are still high, but there is always the possibility that the incentives will stop.
How long does the solar installation process take?
The actual solar panel installation usually takes a few days. From initial estimate until the system is operational usually takes 3 to 6 months including design, permitting, inspections, etc. All this work is tracked in the Solar Powerhouse system.
